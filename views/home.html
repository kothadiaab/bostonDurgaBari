Cultural Program

Indian Classical music by Aditya Shah

Start Time: 6pm

Aditya Rohit Shah has been trained in North Indian Classical Vocal Music under the guidance and blessings of Pandit Rattan Mohan Sharma and Pandit Radharman Kirtane, direct disciples of his Bade-Guruji Sangeet Martand Padmavibhushan Pandit Jasraj-ji. In 2012, Aditya was selected to perform a solo concert for Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, the former President of India. He won First Place in his age category in SwarGanga’s prestigious "Crescendo" National Competition (USA & Canada) in 2013. In 2013, he was deeply honored to receive the prestigious "Mewati Pradeept" award from the Pandit Jasraj Institute, which marked the commencement of his solo musical career.

Aditya has performed at many venues in the States. He is also currently working on several musical projects, including a debut classical album. Boston Durgabari is proud to have him performing on October 1st where he is going to enthrall the audience with his beautiful voice and musical skill.

Drama: Checked-In

Start Time: 7pm

We tease, we laugh, we follow, we vent out, we embrace , we reject but we can not ignore it . Social media has created an evolution in integrated social system and established a spider net of human network. “Checked –In “, an original play written and directed by Jayati Banerjee takes a non-critique and comical view towards human psychology and its adaptive capability under strong influence of social media.

Odissi Dance by Shamoyita

Start Time: 8pm

A versatile Odissi dancer, Shamoyita, has been nurturing her passion in Odissi dance by performing, practicing and teaching the art for about 20 years now. Shamoyita started her tutelage in Odissi with Smt Sushmita Bhattacharya since she was 13. She got a bunch of workshop opportunities in Kolkata and Bhuvaneswar with imminent Odissi gurus in India including Guru Ratikant Mohapatra and Smt Sujata Mohapatra - son and daughter-in-law of Late Guru Kelucharan Mohapatra. After she moved to the US, she has been very fortunate to be associated with the Upasana ensemble and with Smt Mouli Pal. she has been performing for the last 6 years and has started teaching as well.

Her performances include an ICCR invitation by the consulate of Illinois in 2009, the Mass dance festival 2011 and 2012, Maheswari convenstion of North America to name a few. They also include festivals in India like the Disha Festival, Youth festival Swabhumi, All India Gynaecologists conference at Kolkata and several programs at temples and cultural events both in US and India.
